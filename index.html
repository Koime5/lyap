On design

Simplexity, not simplicity

Lyap takes its instincts from Alpine, HTMX, and Svelte — declarative markup, server-friendly interaction, compiled reactivity — and rebuilds them in its own syntax, tuned for one purpose: staying out of the way.

I.

Lightweight

No virtual DOM to ship, no runtime to warm. A single script tag, or a compiler that strips out what you never use.

II.

Scalable

Begin with a touch of interactivity on a static page. Grow into components, stores, and routing without a rewrite in between.

III.

Easy to learn

A handful of directives cover most real interfaces. Familiar within the hour to anyone who has used Alpine or HTMX.

A first look

State and behavior, in the markup

An own directive set and its own compiler — written with the same regard for legibility that drew us to Alpine, HTMX, and Svelte in the first place.

counter.html
<!-- state lives where you use it -->
<div ly:state="{ count: 0 }">

  <button ly:on:click="count++">
    +1
  </button>

  <span ly:text="count"></span>

  <!-- fetch fresh markup, no client router -->
  <button ly:get="/api/bolt" ly:target="#result">
    Strike again
  </button>
  <div id="result"></div>

</div>
